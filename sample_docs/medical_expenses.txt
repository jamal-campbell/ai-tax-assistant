IRS Publication 502 - Medical and Dental Expenses

MEDICAL EXPENSE DEDUCTION

You can deduct qualified medical and dental expenses that exceed 7.5% of your adjusted gross income (AGI). This is an itemized deduction claimed on Schedule A.

Example Calculation:
- AGI: $50,000
- 7.5% threshold: $3,750
- Total medical expenses: $5,000
- Deductible amount: $1,250 ($5,000 - $3,750)

QUALIFIED MEDICAL EXPENSES

Expenses must be primarily for the prevention, diagnosis, treatment, or cure of disease, or for treatments affecting any part or function of the body.

Medical Services:
- Doctors, dentists, surgeons, and specialists
- Hospital services and nursing care
- Laboratory fees and diagnostic tests
- X-rays and imaging
- Physical therapy and rehabilitation
- Mental health treatment and counseling
- Substance abuse treatment

Prescription Items:
- Prescription medications
- Insulin (even without prescription)
- Prescription eyeglasses and contact lenses
- Hearing aids and batteries
- Prosthetic devices

Medical Equipment:
- Wheelchairs and walkers
- Crutches
- Oxygen equipment
- Hospital beds for medical use
- Blood sugar test kits

Other Qualified Expenses:
- Health insurance premiums (if not pre-tax)
- Long-term care insurance premiums (age-based limits)
- Medicare Part B and Part D premiums
- Medigap insurance premiums
- COBRA premiums
- Dental treatment and orthodontia
- Vision correction surgery (LASIK)
- Fertility treatments
- Weight loss programs (if prescribed for specific disease)
- Service animals

TRANSPORTATION FOR MEDICAL CARE

Travel primarily for and essential to medical care is deductible:
- Actual vehicle expenses, or
- Standard mileage rate: 67 cents per mile (2024)
- Plus parking fees and tolls
- Bus, taxi, train, or plane fares
- Ambulance service

Lodging while away from home for medical treatment:
- Up to $50 per night per person
- Must be primarily for medical care
- No significant element of personal pleasure

NON-DEDUCTIBLE EXPENSES

- Cosmetic surgery (unless medically necessary)
- Health club or gym memberships
- Vitamins and supplements (unless prescribed)
- Teeth whitening
- Non-prescription medicines (except insulin)
- Funeral expenses
- Life insurance premiums
- Expenses paid by insurance

HEALTH SAVINGS ACCOUNTS (HSA)

HSA contributions are tax-deductible, and distributions for qualified medical expenses are tax-free.

2024 Contribution Limits:
- Self-only coverage: $4,150
- Family coverage: $8,300
- Catch-up contribution (age 55+): Additional $1,000

Requirements:
- Must be enrolled in a high-deductible health plan (HDHP)
- Cannot be enrolled in Medicare
- Cannot be claimed as a dependent

FLEXIBLE SPENDING ACCOUNTS (FSA)

2024 Contribution Limit: $3,200 per employer plan

Key Features:
- Pre-tax contributions reduce taxable income
- Use-it-or-lose-it rule (with limited rollover or grace period options)
- Can be used for medical, dental, and vision expenses
- Dependent care FSA is separate ($5,000 limit)

SELF-EMPLOYED HEALTH INSURANCE DEDUCTION

Self-employed individuals can deduct 100% of health insurance premiums paid for themselves, spouse, and dependents as an adjustment to income on Schedule 1 (not as itemized deduction).

Requirements:
- Must have net self-employment income
- Cannot be eligible for employer-subsidized health plan
- Deduction limited to net self-employment earnings
