IRS Publication 970 - Tax Benefits for Education

AMERICAN OPPORTUNITY TAX CREDIT (AOTC)

The American Opportunity Tax Credit provides up to $2,500 per eligible student per year for the first four years of higher education.

Credit Calculation:
- 100% of first $2,000 in qualified expenses
- 25% of next $2,000 in qualified expenses
- Maximum credit: $2,500 per student

Refundable Portion:
- 40% of the credit (up to $1,000) is refundable
- Available even if you owe no tax

Eligibility Requirements:
- Student must be pursuing a degree or credential
- Must be enrolled at least half-time for at least one academic period
- Must be in first four years of postsecondary education
- No felony drug conviction

Income Limits (2024):
- Full credit: MAGI up to $80,000 (single) or $160,000 (married filing jointly)
- Phaseout: $80,000-$90,000 (single) or $160,000-$180,000 (MFJ)

LIFETIME LEARNING CREDIT (LLC)

The Lifetime Learning Credit provides up to $2,000 per tax return for qualified education expenses.

Credit Calculation:
- 20% of first $10,000 in qualified expenses
- Maximum credit: $2,000 per tax return (not per student)

Key Features:
- No limit on number of years you can claim
- Available for undergraduate, graduate, and professional degree courses
- Available for courses to improve job skills
- Student does not need to be pursuing a degree

Income Limits (2024):
- Full credit: MAGI up to $80,000 (single) or $160,000 (MFJ)
- Phaseout: $80,000-$90,000 (single) or $160,000-$180,000 (MFJ)

QUALIFIED EDUCATION EXPENSES

For AOTC:
- Tuition and required enrollment fees
- Course materials (books, supplies, equipment)

For LLC:
- Tuition and fees required for enrollment
- Books and supplies only if required to be purchased from institution

NOT Qualified Expenses:
- Room and board
- Insurance
- Medical expenses
- Transportation
- Personal living expenses

COORDINATION RULES

You cannot claim both credits for the same student in the same year. However, you can claim AOTC for one student and LLC for another student on the same return.

Expenses paid with tax-free scholarships, Pell grants, or employer education assistance cannot be used to claim credits.

STUDENT LOAN INTEREST DEDUCTION

You may deduct up to $2,500 in student loan interest paid during the year.

Requirements:
- Loan must be for qualified education expenses
- You must be legally obligated to pay the interest
- Filing status cannot be married filing separately

Income Limits (2024):
- Full deduction: MAGI up to $75,000 (single) or $155,000 (MFJ)
- Phaseout: $75,000-$90,000 (single) or $155,000-$185,000 (MFJ)

529 PLAN DISTRIBUTIONS

Qualified distributions from 529 plans are tax-free when used for:
- Tuition and fees
- Books, supplies, and equipment
- Room and board (if enrolled at least half-time)
- Computer equipment
- K-12 tuition (up to $10,000 per year per beneficiary)
- Student loan repayment (lifetime limit of $10,000)

COVERDELL EDUCATION SAVINGS ACCOUNTS

Annual contribution limit: $2,000 per beneficiary
- Contributions are not deductible
- Earnings grow tax-free
- Distributions tax-free for qualified expenses
- Can be used for K-12 and higher education expenses
