IRS Publication 525 - Taxable and Nontaxable Income: Gambling

GAMBLING WINNINGS

All gambling winnings are fully taxable and must be reported on your tax return. This includes winnings from:
- Casinos and card rooms
- Lotteries and raffles
- Horse racing and dog racing
- Sports betting
- Bingo and slot machines
- Online gambling
- Fantasy sports

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

Form W-2G is issued for certain gambling winnings:
- $1,200 or more from bingo or slot machines
- $1,500 or more from keno (reduced by wager)
- $5,000 or more from poker tournaments (reduced by buy-in)
- $600 or more if at least 300 times the wager (other gambling)

Even if you don't receive a W-2G, you must report all gambling winnings as "Other Income" on Schedule 1 of Form 1040.

GAMBLING LOSSES

You may deduct gambling losses, but only to the extent of gambling winnings. You cannot deduct losses that exceed your winnings.

Key Rules:
- Losses are claimed as an itemized deduction on Schedule A
- You must itemize deductions to claim gambling losses
- Cannot use standard deduction and claim gambling losses
- Losses from one type of gambling can offset winnings from another

Professional Gamblers:
If gambling is your trade or business, report winnings and losses on Schedule C. Professional gamblers can deduct losses up to winnings and may also deduct business expenses (travel, subscriptions, etc.).

RECORDKEEPING REQUIREMENTS

The IRS requires contemporaneous records of gambling activities:

Required Documentation:
- Date and type of gambling activity
- Name and address of gambling establishment
- Names of other persons present
- Amount won or lost

Supporting Documents:
- W-2G forms received
- Wagering tickets and receipts
- Credit card records
- Bank withdrawal slips
- Casino player club statements
- Canceled checks

Diary or Log:
Keep a diary or log showing:
- Each gambling session date
- Type of gambling
- Starting and ending times
- Amounts won and lost per session
- Running totals

WITHHOLDING

Federal income tax is withheld from certain gambling winnings:
- 24% withholding on winnings over $5,000 (reduced by wager) from:
  - Lotteries
  - Sweepstakes
  - Wagering pools
  - Poker tournaments

- 24% backup withholding if you don't provide a valid taxpayer ID

State taxes may also be withheld depending on your state's requirements.

NONRESIDENT ALIENS

Gambling winnings of nonresident aliens from U.S. sources are subject to 30% withholding (or lower treaty rate). Gambling losses cannot be used to offset winnings unless the individual is engaged in a U.S. trade or business.

TAX TREATY BENEFITS

Some tax treaties allow residents of certain countries to reduce or eliminate U.S. tax on gambling winnings. Review the applicable treaty for specific provisions.
